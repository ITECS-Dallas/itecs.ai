## Why This Exists

Section heading:

The wrong way to adopt AI is forever-dependent on outside consultants.

Core message:

Across SMB and mid-market organizations, AI tools are rolling out quickly. The
challenge is not the technology itself. The challenge is long-term capability.
Without an internal champion, AI adoption often launches with excitement, loses
momentum after consultants leave, and turns into shadow AI usage as employees
default to whatever tools they already know.

ITECS built this program to transfer prompt engineering, governance, training,
and platform expertise into one trusted employee so the organization creates AI
capability that compounds over time.

- Building governance and trust: for organizations where legal, compliance, or

## Outcomes

By program completion, the AI Champion should be able to:

- Lead AI platform adoption across multiple departments without external dependency.
- Build, test, and refine production-grade prompts and prompt libraries for the organization's workflows.
- Design and document AI-driven workflows employees can use day to day.
- Train new employees using a curriculum tailored to the environment.
- Identify, evaluate, and prioritize new AI use cases based on business impact and feasibility.
- Enforce the AI Acceptable Use Policy and detect shadow AI usage.
- Evaluate major AI platforms including Claude, ChatGPT, Gemini, Copilot, and others.
- Coordinate with ITECS or other outside resources when senior judgment is required.

## Curriculum

Section heading:

A structured 4 to 6 week curriculum, tailored to your business.

Intro:

Every Champion's curriculum is customized to the AI platforms, industry, and use
cases that matter to the organization. The standard version is six weeks. The
four-week version compresses Weeks 5 and 6 into the weekly cadence and is ideal
for organizations that already have an established AI deployment.

Weeks:

- Week 1, Foundation and Goal-Setting: AI fundamentals, selected platform deep
  dive, workspace orientation, governance walkthrough, and goal-setting.
- Week 2, Real-Work Prompt Engineering: shadowing an ITECS AI Strategist on live
  prompt work and building the first three production-quality prompts.
- Week 3, Workflow and Library Design: organizing document corpora, project
  workspaces, and role-based prompt libraries.
- Week 4, Train-the-Trainer: onboarding methods, handling questions from
  non-technical staff, executive updates, and practice running training.
- Week 5, Expansion and Measurement: prioritizing new use cases, measuring ROI,
  and building the next two quarters of expansion.
- Week 6, Governance and Sustainability: acceptable use enforcement, shadow AI
  monitoring, vendor evaluation, and the 90-day handoff plan.

Time commitment:

Each week includes about 3-5 hours of synchronous time with the ITECS AI
Strategist and 4-6 hours of applied work.
